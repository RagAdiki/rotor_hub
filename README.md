# rotor_hub
This project focuses on the design and development of a swashplateless micro-helicopter inspired by the Black Hornet nano-UAV, with the goal of achieving full cyclic control without conventional swashplate mechanisms. The core innovation lies in a custom rotor hub where lag and flap degrees of freedom are linearly coupled through geometric offsets and skewed hinge elements, enabling passive aeroelastic pitch control.
The work began with deriving the full coupled lag–flap–pitch equations of motion on Mathematica, followed by nonlinear simulation in MATLAB/Simulink to characterise rotor dynamics under varying structural coupling coefficients. Sinusoidal excitation studies under hover trim conditions were used to validate dynamic stability margins and confirm control bandwidth.
On the hardware side, a magnetic encoder was integrated to track blade azimuthal position in real time, synchronising the sinusoidal pitch input to blade azimuth to achieve controlled tilt of the rotor disc. Multiple ESC platforms — VESC, BLHeli_S, and BLHeli_32 — were evaluated alongside PWM, OneShot, and DShot signal protocols for high-bandwidth motor control. A bench-top test rig was built and instrumented to measure thrust, torque, and blade flapping, with results iterated against simulation. FEA was additionally performed on the airframe to validate structural integrity and dynamic stability through modal analysis.
